Prime Minister of Pakistan

Imran Khan, born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, is a former world-renowned cricketer, philanthropist, and politician. He rose to fame as the captain of Pakistan’s national cricket team, leading the country to its first-ever Cricket World Cup victory in 1992. After retiring from cricket, he turned to philanthropy and founded the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital and Namal University, both aimed at providing affordable health and education services. In 1996, he entered politics by establishing the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party, focusing on justice, anti-corruption, and reforms. Imran Khan became the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan in 2018 but was removed from office through a no-confidence vote in April 2022. Since then, he has faced several legal challenges and was sentenced to jail in various cases. Despite this, he remains a highly influential and popular figure in Pakistani politics.
